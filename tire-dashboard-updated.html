Key Market Insights

  • Operational Efficiency: Gambia shows lower operational costs despite higher shipping costs, resulting in a 2.60% higher overall margin.
  • Market Specialization: Gambia performs better with small passenger tires (13"-14") and commercial tires, while Senegal shows advantages for mid-range passenger tires.
  • Price Divergence: Heavy commercial tires (22.5") show significant price variation between markets, with Senegal commanding 26-36% higher prices.
  • Container Shipping: Gambia's container shipping costs are consistently $200-400 higher than Senegal due to port fees and less shipping competition.
  • Seasonal Factors: Both markets show similar seasonality patterns, with peak demand during March-May and September-November.

Develop stronger distribution network and consider hub-and-spoke model using Senegal as regional distribution center.

Strategic Recommendations

Actionable insights to maximize profitability across both markets

The following recommendations are based on comprehensive cost analysis and market-specific trends. Implementation priority is indicated for each recommendation.

Pricing Optimization

Immediate Price Adjustments
Increase prices for problematic sizes (195/75R16, 215/75R16, 255/55R19) by 25-30% to achieve minimum 10% margins. The 255/55R19 size is particularly critical with a current -25.11% margin and requires immediate action.
Market-Specific Pricing
Implement differential pricing between Gambia and Senegal based on operational costs and competitive dynamics. Maintain premium pricing for commercial tires in Gambia while offering more competitive pricing for passenger tires in Senegal.
Segmented Pricing Strategy
Develop tiered pricing structures for different customer segments, particularly for high-margin commercial tires. Offer volume-based incentives for fleet customers while maintaining premium pricing for individual purchases.

Supply Chain Optimization

Origin-Specific Sourcing
Source all commercial tires (17.5", 19.5", 22.5") from Europe/Middle East to maximize margins by 5.75-7.50%. For passenger tires, source 13"-16" from Europe/Middle East (2.17-3.27% margin improvement) and 17"-21" from America when Europe/Middle East isn't available.
Container Utilization
Implement mixed-size container loading to maximize space and weight utilization. Combine high-margin commercial tires with complementary passenger sizes to optimize overall container profitability.
Seasonal Shipping Strategy
Concentrate shipping during January-March when freight rates are 5-10% lower. Build strategic inventory during these periods to reduce dependency on peak season (August-November) when rates are 10-20% higher.

Inventory Strategy

Focus on High-Margin Categories
Prioritize inventory investment in commercial tire categories (17.5", 19.5", 22.5") which maintain strong 34-60% margins even after comprehensive cost allocation. Within passenger tires, focus on 15", 16", and 19" categories with margins exceeding 35%.
Problem Size Management
Consider gradually phasing out problematic sizes with persistently low margins (below 10%) if price increases aren't viable. Alternatively, negotiate better purchasing terms with suppliers specifically for these sizes.
Regional Inventory Distribution
Allocate commercial tire inventory primarily to Gambia where margins are stronger, while distributing passenger tire inventory with a slight bias toward Senegal for mid-range sizes (15"-17"). Maintain cross-border inventory flexibility to respond to market opportunities.

Operational Improvements

Cost Structure Management
Continue monitoring the balance between bundling/packing costs and contingency. The recent shift of $0.75 from bundling to contingency helps manage risk while maintaining operational efficiency.
Market-Specific Operations
Leverage Gambia's lower operational costs for labor-intensive activities while utilizing Senegal's better port infrastructure for high-volume shipments. Consider establishing a regional hub in Senegal with distribution to Gambia for certain categories.
Efficiency Improvements
Implement operational improvements to reduce handling costs, particularly for commercial tires where margins are strongest. Focus on semi-automated bundling processes and optimized container loading procedures.

  2. Short-term (1-3 months): Implement origin-specific sourcing strategy and begin container optimization
  3. Medium-term (3-6 months): Develop market-specific inventory distribution and bundling strategies
